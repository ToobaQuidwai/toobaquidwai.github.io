Research profile

Editorial overview of the work, training, and scientific focus areas drawn directly from the resume.

Her PhD at the University of Edinburgh identified a coatomer-like vesicle transport role for WDR35/IFT121, combining 3D-TEM, correlative microscopy, mouse genetics, and proteomics to resolve how membrane cargo reaches the cilium.

Postdoctoral work at UMass Chan Medical School expanded that program into mouse models, Chlamydomonas biology, kidney cystogenesis, and central apparatus biology, while earlier training at EMBL Heidelberg and the Max Planck Institute of Molecular Physiology built a strong foundation in super-resolution imaging, signaling, and quantitative cell biology.

Core strengths

  • Advanced imaging with super-resolution microscopy, CLEM, and electron microscopy.
  • Cross-disciplinary research spanning ciliary biology, proteomics, genomics, and disease models.
  • International collaboration across India, Germany, the UK, the USA, and current Copenhagen-linked grant efforts.
  • Public engagement through invited scientific talks and outreach in India and abroad.
