Researching cilia, imaging cellular architecture, and linking mechanism to disease.

Dr. Tooba Quidwai is a molecular and cell biologist with more than a decade of international research experience across India, Germany, the United Kingdom, and the United States. Her work integrates cilia biology, vesicular trafficking, proteomics, genomics, super-resolution microscopy, and translational disease models to study how cellular architecture shapes pathology in kidney disease, metabolic disorders, and related ciliopathies.

Research profile

A concise research statement focused on ciliary transport, imaging-led biology, and disease-relevant cell architecture.

During her PhD at the University of Edinburgh, Dr. Quidwai identified a coatomer-like role for WDR35/IFT121 in ciliary membrane cargo transport, integrating 3D-TEM, correlative microscopy, mouse genetics, and proteomics to explain how membrane cargo reaches the cilium.

Her postdoctoral work at UMass Chan Medical School expanded that framework into kidney cystogenesis, ADAMTS9-mediated TMEM67 biology, central apparatus function, and Chlamydomonas-based systems, while earlier appointments at EMBL Heidelberg and the Max Planck Institute established a strong foundation in super-resolution imaging, signaling, and quantitative cell biology.

What defines the work

  • Mechanistic cilia biology tied directly to disease phenotypes.
  • Imaging as a discovery tool, not just a visualization layer.
  • Cross-system work spanning mammalian cells, mouse models, and Chlamydomonas.
  • International collaboration across India, Germany, the UK, the USA, and Copenhagen-linked grant efforts.
